# Part A: Identify Missing Components

| Missing Component | Why It's Important |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Header & Document Control** | Establishes official status, version management, classification, and metadata ownership. |
| **Purpose Statement** | Explains why the policy exists and the organizational risks it intends to mitigate. |
| **Scope Definition** | Defines exactly who (employees, contractors) and what systems are bound by the rules. |
| **Specific Policy Statements** | Provides clear, unambiguous, and measurable technical/behavioral rules. |
| **Roles & Responsibilities** | Assigns accountability for implementing, managing, and complying with the policy. |
| **Enforcement Section** | Deters non-compliance by establishing formal administrative or legal consequences. |
| **Definitions & References** | Eliminates confusion over technical terms and links the policy to actionable procedures. |
| **Revision History** | Tracks policy iterations and approvals to ensure compliance with compliance audits. |

# Part B: Identify Weaknesses

| Weakness (Quote) | Problem | Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **"should use"** | Permissive and non-mandatory language instead of a requirement. | Compliance becomes optional; employees can choose to ignore the guideline without violation. |
| **"good passwords"** | Subjective and completely unmeasurable terminology. | Users will set weak passwords thinking they meet the vague standard. |
| **"IT will handle security stuff"** | Ambiguous scope, responsibility allocation, and unprofessional language. | Leads to operational gaps, lack of accountability, and critical tasks being missed. |
| **"Report problems to someone"** | No defined contact, reporting mechanism, or timeline for incidents. | Security breaches go unreported or are sent to the wrong people, increasing damage. |
| **"Updated: Sometime last year"** | Lacks precise date, version tracking, control ID, and formal approval stamp. | The document is legally unenforceable and its validity cannot be verified during an audit. |

### 1. Purpose
This policy establishes the baseline security rules required to protect company data, information systems, and technology infrastructure from internal and external threats.

### 2. Scope
* **2.1 Applicability:** Applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, and third-party vendors.
* **2.2 Covered Assets:** All corporate data, devices, local networks, and cloud applications.
* **2.3 Exclusions:** None.

### 3. Policy Statements
* **3.1 Access Control & Passwords:** Users must secure corporate accounts with strong passwords (minimum 12 characters) and use Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) where available. Credentials must never be shared.
* **3.2 Asset Protection:** Corporate endpoints, laptops, and networks must be protected from unauthorized physical or digital exposure. Software may only be installed from authorized repositories.
* **3.3 Incident Reporting:** Any suspected data breach, lost corporate device, or unusual system behavior must be reported immediately to the IT Security Team.

### 4. Roles and Responsibilities
* **Executive Management:** Approves policy initiatives and allocates security resources.
* **IT Security Team:** Deploys safeguards, monitors network behavior, and manages incident tracking.
* **Department Managers:** Ensures teams complete training and enforce daily operational compliance.
* **All Users:** Safely utilizes corporate assets and instantly flags security anomalies.

### 5. Compliance
* **5.1 Monitoring:** Automated systems log network activity and audit compliance parameters.
* **5.2 Reporting:** Monthly security summaries are sent to the Information Security Committee.
* **5.3 Auditing:** Periodic, unannounced technical audits verify policy alignment across systems.

### 6. Enforcement
* **6.1 Violations:** Deviating from these mandates may lead to revocation of network access, disciplinary warnings, or immediate termination.
* **6.2 Reporting Violations:** Send notifications immediately to security@company.com.

### 7. Exceptions
Exceptions require a written business case, a formal risk evaluation, and written approval by the CISO.

### 8. Definitions
* **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** A verification protocol requiring two or more independent credentials.

### 9. Related Documents
Information Security Framework; Identity & Access Management Standard; Incident Response Plan.
